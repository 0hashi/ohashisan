My first encounter with the dark web was an eye-opener to say the least, and my first glimpse into that rabbit hole left me with more questions than answers. How can such a place that is accessible to anyone with an internet connection exist, blows my mind.

For me, it all started when I learned about The Onion Router (Tor). Tor uses a method of secure relays designed to encrypt and pass traffic across the internet. A proxy assigns you your IP server's address rather than your own, a sleight of hand that obfuscates your online activities. This "sleight of hand", or chain of proxy relays is what provides a level of anonymity that cannot be achieved with a VPN, alone.

So, should you use a VPN if you're surfing the Dark Web with Tor? That's a question many people are floating around the internet, and I have not yet found a winning "technical" argument. As for myself, I use a VPN when I explore the Dark Web, because my ISP seems to have started blocking access to onion sites.
